Other useful programs

  • Theoretical scattering from formfactors can be calculated, e.g., by SasView or SasFit or Irena.
  • The above programs can also be used for fitting SAS data with formfactors, structure factors and many other analytics (see respective links).
  • The scattering from arbitrarily shaped particles can be calculated via the Debye sum using SPONGE or CDEF. SPONGE and CDEF are less accessible than Shape2SAS, but more accurate. Importantly, they also allow for fitting of data, so they can be used directly in data analysis.
  • The size distribution of a sample measured with SAXS or SANS can be analyzed with McSAS or McSAS3.
